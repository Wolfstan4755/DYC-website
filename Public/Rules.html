DYC Guidelines and Rules

As members of the Diocesan Youth Council, we follow these rules to ensure a positive, respectful, and Christ-centered community.

General Rules

  • Respect for Others: All members must show respect for each other, staff, and the wider community. Bullying, harassment, or any form of disrespect will not be tolerated.
  • Participation: Active participation is encouraged. Members should attend meetings and events regularly and contribute to discussions and activities.
  • Modesty: Follow appropriate dress codes for meetings and events, keeping in mind the Catholic values of modesty and decency.
  • Faith-Centered Behavior: Strive to reflect Christ’s love in your actions, words, and behavior both inside and outside of Council activities.

Meeting Conduct

  • Punctuality: Arrive on time for all meetings and events. If you are unable to attend, notify a leader in advance.
  • Focus: During meetings, stay focused on the agenda. Avoid distractions such as phones or unrelated conversations.
  • Respectful Communication: When speaking, be respectful of others’ opinions and listen attentively when others are sharing.
  • Service and Leadership: As a member, you are encouraged to take initiative in serving the group and fulfilling leadership roles as needed.

Event Guidelines

  • Behavior at Events: Represent the DYC with integrity. Remember, your actions reflect our faith and community values.
  • Safety: Follow all safety guidelines set by the event coordinators. Be aware of your surroundings and take care of yourself and others.
  • Permission Forms: For certain events, you may need parental consent or permission forms. Ensure these are submitted on time.
  • Volunteering: Members are encouraged to volunteer at diocesan events. Volunteering opportunities will be communicated in advance.

Disciplinary Actions

Failure to comply with these rules may result in disciplinary actions, which could include warnings, temporary suspension from events, or removal from the Council depending on the severity of the violation.
